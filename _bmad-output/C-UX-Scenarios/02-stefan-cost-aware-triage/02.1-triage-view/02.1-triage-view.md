## Overview

**Page Purpose:** Same surface as [01.1-triage-view](../../01-stefan-crisis-redeployment/01.1-triage-view/01.1-triage-view.md) — the difference is *the user behaviour path*. In scenario 01, a crisis modal fires unprompted. In scenario 02, no crisis fires; Stefan **scans the feed and clicks a non-blocking WARNING card to explore it.** All structural specs (layout, spacing, typography, sections) are identical to 01.1 and inherit it by reference.

**User Situation:** Stefan is mid-shift, mid-afternoon, no active crisis. A WARNING card surfaces in the feed: "Unit 4831 — brake wear approaching threshold." It is not blocking service. He has bandwidth to investigate.

**Success Criteria:**
- WARNING cards visibly distinct from CRITICAL (severity-warning band, not severity-critical)
- Cost-impact one-liner on WARNING card communicates *potential* impact, not just sunk cost
- Card responds to click with the same expand affordance regardless of severity

**Entry Points:**
- Default page after login (same as 01.1)

**Exit Points:**
- Stefan clicks a card (any severity, non-crisis-modal-firing) → [02.2-card-expand](../02.2-card-expand/02.2-card-expand.md)
- CRITICAL severity with `request_landside_tasking: true` arrives → [01.2-crisis-modal](../../01-stefan-crisis-redeployment/01.2-crisis-modal/01.2-crisis-modal.md) (scenario 01 flow)

## Cost-Impact One-Liner — WARNING variant

The card's cost-impact one-liner uses different framing for WARNING vs CRITICAL cards.

| Severity | Cost-impact one-liner shape | Example |
|---|---|---|
| CRITICAL | Current sunk + ongoing | "Service disrupted: ~14 min delay accumulating" |
| WARNING | Forecasted impact + window | "Estimated maintenance window: 4h · cost €380 · delay impact if not scheduled: ~12 min/affected service over 2 weeks" |
| INFO | Observational | "Approaching threshold — no immediate action required" |
| RESOLVED | Historical | "Cleared — total impact: 14 min delay, resolved manually" |

The shape is server-side computed (pre-rendered into the card payload), not client-side LLM.
